Important Concepts

What is a Variable?

A variable is a storage location, identified by a name, that holds a value in a program.

What is a Function?

A function is a reusable block of code that performs a specific task.

Paradigms in HCI

HCI paradigms are foundational approaches that guide how researchers and designers understand, study, and create interactions between humans and technology. The three main paradigms are: Human Factors, which emphasizes physical usability and safety; Information Processing, which focuses on optimizing mental processes and user performance; and Phenomenologically Situated, which explores real-world, lived experiences and social contexts of technology use. Together, these paradigms offer complementary perspectives for creating effective, efficient, and meaningful human-computer interactions.

Paradigms in HCI VR Focussed Classification Example Questions/Studies
Human Factors (Ergonomics & Safety) Physical comfort, usability, and safety in VR Is the VR headset comfortable to wear for long periods? Are users experiencing motion sickness or strain?
Information Processing (Cognitive Efficiency) Mental workload and task performance in VR How effectively can users navigate and complete tasks in a VR environment? Does the VR interface help users make quick, accurate decisions?
Phenomenologically Situated (Lived Experience) Real-world, contextual VR experiences How do users actually engage with VR in their daily lives? How does VR change social interaction or habits? What meaning do users attach to their VR experiences?
